Classic Nigerian Jollof Rice

A vibrant West African dish of rice cooked in a rich blend of tomatoes, peppers, and spices. Loved at parties, family gatherings, and special occasions across Nigeria.

Recipe Information

Preparation time: 20 minutes

Cooking time: 45 minutes

Servings: 6

Difficulty: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 4 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 6 medium tomatoes (blended)
  • 4 red bell peppers (blended)
  • 2 medium onions (1 blended, 1 sliced)
  • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons ground paprika
  • 2 teaspoons curry powder
  • 2 teaspoons thyme
  • 4 cups chicken stock
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Rinse rice thoroughly and set aside.
  2. Blend tomatoes, red bell peppers, and 1 onion until smooth.
  3. Heat oil in a pot, add sliced onions, and fry until fragrant.
  4. Add tomato paste and stir-fry for 2 minutes to reduce acidity.
  5. Pour in blended mixture, season with paprika, curry, thyme, salt, and pepper, and cook until sauce thickens.
  6. Add chicken stock and bring to a boil.
  7. Stir in rice, reduce heat, cover tightly, and cook until rice absorbs sauce.
  8. Fluff with a fork and serve hot.

Tips

Tip: For a smoky party-style flavor, allow the rice at the bottom to toast slightly without burning. Always use fresh ingredients for the richest taste.
